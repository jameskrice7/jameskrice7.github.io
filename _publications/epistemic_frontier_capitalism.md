This article advances a theory of epistemic frontier capitalism, arguing that under the pressure of frontier artificial intelligence and platform-mediated misinformation, accumulation, growth, and knowledge production are increasingly shaped by the privatization and strategic destabilization of shared epistemic commons.

The paper develops this thesis through linked case studies of artificial intelligence and misinformation as strategies of capital accumulation. It argues that capitalism increasingly profits from corroding the epistemic and intellectual preconditions of its own vitality through information warfare, while artificial intelligence industrializes inference and concentrates power over decision-making authority.
